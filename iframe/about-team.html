Methodology

We used an Agile methodology for fast prototyping and an incremental development. We run weekly meetings for tasks assignation, review of the latests developments and brainstorming.

Coordination

The team was divided into several roles. The team leader was in charge of setting up the meetings, debriefing the team, tasks assignation and motivation. The decisions making was done by majority vote. In case of tie the Team Leader had the decision vote.

    • Lessons learned

    Meetings
  • Finding time-slots that fits in everyones schedule not easy.
  • Agreeing on one tool to support scheduling difficult.
  • Building sub-groups of two was a pleasurable experience.
  • Joaquín did a great job documenting meetings.
  • Due to large team-size and time constraints I kept myself quiet and didn't bring up every aspect I would have liked to.
    • Data handling
  • Raw data base is scarce and fragmented.
  • Existant data sources not always carefully maintained.
  • Lack of publicly available database.
  • Lack of Open Data in intersection of nutrition and environment.
  • Raw data divert a lot wrt to scope and apporach.
  • Data sources covering ""all relevant issues"" missing.
  • Standards and requirements/scope vary a lot between countries and geographic regions.
  • Postive: requests for support to EFSA, e.g., were attended in kind and fast manner, mostly helpful.
  • Accessibility not always easy investigate.
    • Website
  • Data preparation is crucial (compilation/presentation of special characters).
    • Graphic design
  • Immediate idea not always suitable.
  • Individual differences in taste and esthetic preferences.
    • Presentations
  • It is beneficial to have not only one team-member doing the whole presentation.
  • Some small issues are not being attended to if only one is in charge.
  • Improved proficiency in handling animations.
    • Tests
  • Systematic approach with literature research and approach adjusted to domain of Information Visualization time intensive.
  • Evaluation of existing and accessible tools for doing remote user testing insightful.
  • Wish do avoid Google Forms was not implemented in the end.
  • Pragmatic discount usability testing and quick-and-dirty approach finally won.
  • Finding and convincing users to test w/o special incentives difficulty.
  • Proper evaluation with original target group (novices, untrained users) failed due to complexity of test tasks and test design.
  • Several adjustments were necessary and end-result not satisfying (many principles violated).
  • Novice users were very critical, esp wrt graphic design.
  • Remote user tests have advantages (time), but disadvantages became also obvious.
    • Coordination
  • In the beginning I had a hard time to get familiar with the various online tools used to chat, share calendars, do presentations, track tasks, do polls, follow announcements.
  • Main insight: if there is one accepted project leader that takes care of every team member like Joaquín did in an exemplary way goals can be achieved in a very efficient way.
  • Joaquín represented a very positive role-model compared to past experiences, where often dominant group members took over command just to shine themselves and didn't care about individual team members very much (ego-shooter approach).
  • One main problem that occurs again and again in team projects completed with limited time resources: every team member does what he/she alywas do and there is no learning effect.
  • I would have liked to learn more on development but I would have be a handicap to the group members that already had a certain level of experience, so I concentrated on what I am proficient in doing already.
